When Can You Upgrade Your O2 Phone? Understanding Eligibility

One of the most common questions we receive at O2 Helpdesk is: "When am I eligible to upgrade?" The answer depends on several factors, including the type of plan you're on, how long you've had your current device, and whether you're willing to pay any early upgrade fees. Let's break down the eligibility criteria in detail.

Standard Upgrade Eligibility

For most O2 customers on traditional 24-month contracts, you'll become eligible for a standard upgrade once you've completed the minimum term of your agreement. This typically means:

  • 24-month contracts: Eligible for upgrade after 24 months
  • 36-month contracts: Eligible for upgrade after 36 months
  • O2 Refresh plans: Eligible to upgrade once your Device Plan is paid off

O2 Refresh: The Flexible Upgrade Option

O2 Refresh has revolutionised how customers upgrade their phones. Unlike traditional contracts where you're locked in for the full term, O2 Refresh separates your bill into two parts: the Device Plan (the cost of your phone) and the Airtime Plan (your minutes, texts, and data).

With O2 Refresh, you can upgrade as soon as you've paid off your Device Plan. This could be:

  • At the end of your original term (typically 24 or 36 months)
  • Earlier if you choose to pay off your remaining Device Plan balance
  • Anytime by trading in your current device to offset the remaining balance

Early Upgrade Options

Can't wait for your standard eligibility date? O2 offers early upgrade options, though these typically come with additional costs. The exact amount depends on:

  • How many months remain on your current contract
  • The remaining balance on your Device Plan (for O2 Refresh customers)
  • Whether you're willing to trade in your current device
  • Any special promotions O2 may be running

What to Do Before Upgrading: Essential Preparation

Before you upgrade to your new O2 phone, there are several important steps to take to ensure a smooth transition. Proper preparation will protect your data, make the switch easier, and help you get the most value from your trade-in.

Step 1: Back Up Your Data

This is the most critical step. Ensure all your important data is safely backed up:

For iPhone Users:

  • iCloud Backup: Go to Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud > iCloud Backup > Back Up Now
  • iTunes/Finder Backup: Connect to computer and create an encrypted backup
  • Photos: Ensure all photos are in iCloud Photos or backed up separately
  • Contacts: Sync with iCloud to ensure nothing is lost

For Android Users:

  • Google Backup: Go to Settings > System > Backup > Back Up Now
  • Samsung Cloud: For Samsung devices, use Samsung Cloud backup
  • Google Photos: Back up all photos and videos
  • Contacts: Ensure synced with Google account

Step 2: Document Your Trade-In Device

If you're trading in your current phone:

  • Take photos of your device from all angles
  • Document the IMEI number (dial *#06#)
  • Note any existing damage or wear
  • Keep a record of your trade-in valuation

Step 3: Remove Accounts and Security

  • iPhone: Turn off Find My iPhone and sign out of iCloud
  • Android: Remove Google account and disable Find My Device
  • Samsung: Remove Samsung account
  • Sign out of all apps and services
  • Remove any screen locks or PINs

Step 4: Factory Reset Your Device

Once everything is backed up and accounts removed:

iPhone Factory Reset:

  1. Go to Settings > General > Transfer or Reset iPhone
  2. Select "Erase All Content and Settings"
  3. Enter your passcode and Apple ID password when prompted
  4. Wait for the process to complete

Android Factory Reset:

  1. Go to Settings > System > Reset Options
  2. Select "Erase All Data (Factory Reset)"
  3. Confirm and enter your PIN/password
  4. Wait for the process to complete

Step 5: Prepare Your SIM

  • Decide if you need a new SIM or will use your existing one
  • If keeping your number, ensure your new SIM is activated correctly
  • If using eSIM, request activation details from O2
  • Keep your old SIM until your new phone is fully working

Step 6: Gather Accessories

  • Check if your new phone comes with a charger (many don't)
  • Order a case and screen protector in advance
  • Consider if you need new cables or adapters
  • Keep your old accessories as backups

Keeping Your Number When Upgrading

One of the biggest concerns when upgrading is keeping your existing phone number. The good news is that if you're upgrading with O2 and staying on O2, keeping your number is straightforward. However, if you're switching from another network, you'll need to follow the PAC code process.

Keeping Your Number with O2 to O2 Upgrades

If you're already an O2 customer and upgrading to a new O2 plan:

  • Your number will transfer automatically to your new SIM
  • No PAC code is required
  • The transfer typically happens within 24 hours of activation
  • Your old SIM will stop working once the transfer is complete

Transferring from Another Network to O2

If you're switching to O2 from a different network:

  1. Request a PAC code: Text PAC to 65075 from your current phone (free)
  2. Receive your PAC: You'll get your PAC code within 60 seconds, valid for 30 days
  3. Provide to O2: Give your PAC code to O2 when ordering or setting up
  4. Choose transfer date: Select when you want the transfer to happen
  5. Wait for completion: Transfers typically complete within one working day

Important Things to Remember

  • Don't cancel your old contract: Let the transfer process handle this automatically
  • Keep your old SIM active: Until the transfer is confirmed complete
  • Check your new phone works: Make and receive a test call after transfer
  • Update your contacts: Let important contacts know if you have a new temporary number

What If Something Goes Wrong?

If your number transfer doesn't work as expected:

  • Contact O2 customer service immediately
  • Have your PAC code and both SIM details ready
  • Don't try to fix it yourself—let O2 handle the technical side
  • Most issues are resolved within 24 hours

3. What is O2 Refresh and how does it work?

O2 Refresh splits your bill into a Device Plan (phone cost) and Airtime Plan (minutes, texts, data). You can upgrade once your Device Plan is paid off, either at the end of the term or earlier by paying the remaining balance.

4. Can I upgrade early with O2?

Yes, but costs depend on your plan type. O2 Refresh customers pay their remaining Device Plan balance. Traditional contract customers may pay early upgrade fees based on remaining months.

5. How much does an early O2 upgrade cost?

For O2 Refresh, it's your remaining Device Plan balance minus any trade-in value. For traditional contracts, fees vary based on months remaining. Contact us for a personalised calculation.

6. Can I trade in my old phone with O2?

Yes, O2 offers a trade-in programme where you can exchange your current device for credit towards your upgrade. The value depends on your device's model, condition, and age.

7. How is my trade-in value calculated?

Trade-in value is based on your device model, storage capacity, physical condition, screen condition, functionality, and current market demand. Newer devices in excellent condition fetch the highest values.

8. What happens to my old phone when I trade it in?

O2 inspects your device to confirm its condition matches your description. The trade-in value is then applied as credit to your account or new device purchase. Devices are refurbished, resold, or recycled responsibly.

9. Can I keep my phone number when upgrading?

If upgrading from O2 to O2, your number transfers automatically. If switching from another network, you'll need a PAC code from your current provider.

10. What's the difference between O2 Essential, Extra, and Unlimited plans?

Essential offers basic data with no extras. Extra includes Priority access, roaming, and one Extra. Unlimited provides unlimited data with multiple Extras and all premium features.

11. What are O2 Extras?

Extras are bundled services like Amazon Prime, Disney+, Audible, or extra data that come free with Extra and Unlimited plans. You can choose the Extra that best suits your needs.

12. What is O2 Priority and how do I access it?

O2 Priority is a rewards programme offering exclusive deals, early ticket access, free weekly treats, and discounts. Download the O2 Priority app and log in with your My O2 credentials to access benefits.

13. What are Volt benefits?

Volt benefits are exclusive perks for customers who have both O2 mobile and Virgin Media services, including double mobile data, broadband speed boosts, and enhanced Wi-Fi guarantees.

14. How do I back up my phone before upgrading?

iPhone users should use iCloud or iTunes backup. Android users should use Google Backup or Samsung Cloud. Ensure photos, contacts, and important data are safely stored before resetting your device.

15. Can I upgrade my business phones with O2?

Yes, O2 offers dedicated business plans with shared data, business apps, priority support, and flexible upgrade options. Contact us for business-specific upgrade advice.

16. What's the best time to upgrade for the best deals?

Black Friday, Boxing Day sales, and new phone launch periods typically offer the best deals. End of financial quarters can also bring promotions as sales targets approach.

17. Can I downgrade my plan when upgrading?

Yes, you can choose any available plan when upgrading. If you're using less data than your current allowance, downgrading to Essential could save you money.

18. Do I need a new SIM when upgrading?

Not always. If your current SIM fits your new phone and you're staying on O2, you can keep it. However, new SIMs are often provided and may be required for 5G or eSIM activation.

19. How long does an O2 upgrade take?

Online upgrades can be completed immediately. In-store upgrades are instant. Phone orders typically arrive next-day. Number transfers usually complete within 24 hours.

20. What if I change my mind after upgrading?

O2 offers a 14-day cooling-off period for distance sales (online/phone). You can return your device and cancel the contract within this period, though usage charges may apply.
