Technical Support

Poor signal or no reception can be frustrating, but there are several steps you can take to diagnose and potentially resolve the issue. First, check if there's a known network outage in your area by visiting the O2 service status page or checking the My O2 app, which displays current network conditions and any reported problems.

If no outage is reported, try these troubleshooting steps:

  • Restart your device completely by powering it off and on again
  • Toggle Airplane Mode on for 30 seconds, then turn it off to refresh your network connection
  • Check your SIM card is properly inserted and clean
  • Try manually selecting the O2 network in your phone's settings
  • Ensure your phone's software is up to date

Signal strength can vary based on your location, building materials, weather conditions, and network congestion. If you consistently experience poor signal at home or work, we may be able to provide a signal booster or recommend alternative solutions. Our network team continuously works to improve coverage across the UK.

Still having signal issues? Our technical team can run advanced diagnostics. Call 01202 925102

Setting up mobile data on your O2 phone is typically automatic when you insert your SIM card, but if you're experiencing issues, we can help you configure it correctly. For most smartphones, mobile data should work immediately once your SIM is activated. You can verify this by checking that mobile data is enabled in your phone's settings.

To manually configure your internet settings:

  1. Go to Settings > Mobile Network > Access Point Names (Android) or Settings > Mobile Data > Mobile Data Network (iPhone)
  2. Ensure the APN is set to "idata.o2.co.uk" for pay monthly or "payandgo.o2.co.uk" for Pay As You Go
  3. Leave username and password fields blank
  4. Save settings and restart your device

If you're still unable to connect, try resetting your network settings or contact us for device-specific guidance. We can also send configuration settings directly to your phone via text message. Remember that data usage counts towards your allowance, so keep an eye on your usage through the My O2 app to avoid unexpected charges.

Slow mobile internet can be caused by various factors, from network congestion to device settings. First, check your current signal strength – a weak signal will naturally result in slower speeds. If you're switching to a different network and want to keep your current phone number, you'll need a PAC (Porting Authorisation Code). If you'd prefer a new number with your new provider, you'll need a STAC (Service Termination Authorisation Code). O2 is required by law to provide these codes immediately upon request.

Getting your code is simple:

  • Text to Switch: Text "PAC" to 65075 for a PAC code, or "STAC" to 75075 for a STAC code
  • Online: Request through the My O2 app or online account
  • By phone: Call our customer service team

Your PAC or STAC code will be provided within 60 seconds via text message and remains valid for 30 days. Once you give this code to your new provider, they'll handle the transfer process, which typically takes one working day. Your O2 service will remain active until the port is complete, ensuring no loss of service. If you change your mind after requesting a code, simply don't use it – it will expire automatically after 30 days with no impact on your O2 service.
