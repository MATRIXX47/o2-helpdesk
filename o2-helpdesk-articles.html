In this detailed guide, we'll explore the ten most common reasons for unexpectedly high O2 bills, help you identify which applies to your situation, and provide actionable solutions to prevent it happening again.

1. Out-of-Bundle Charges

The most frequent culprit behind high bills is exceeding your monthly allowances. When you use more minutes, texts, or data than your plan includes, O2 charges premium out-of-bundle rates.

How to Check:

  • Log into My O2 app and view your usage breakdown
  • Look for sections labelled "Extra charges" or "Out of bundle"
  • Check if you've exceeded data, minutes, or text allowances

Solution:

Set up usage alerts in My O2 to warn you when approaching your limits. Consider upgrading to a plan with higher allowances if this happens regularly.

2. International Roaming Charges

Using your phone abroad without the correct roaming package can result in astronomical charges. Even receiving calls or texts while overseas can incur fees.

O2 Travel destinations £6/day for unlimited data Buy O2 Travel Bolt On
Rest of World Up to £8.10 per MB of data Buy local SIM or use Wi-Fi

3. Premium Rate Numbers and Services

Calls to numbers starting with 084, 087, 09, or 118 can cost significantly more than standard calls. Additionally, subscribing to premium text services (like competitions or horoscopes) adds charges to your bill.

Common Premium Services to Watch For:

  • Directory enquiries (118 numbers)
  • Technical support lines for non-O2 products
  • TV voting lines
  • Competition entries via text
  • Subscription services you've forgotten about

4. Device Plan Increases

If you're on O2 Refresh, your bill is split into an Airtime Plan and a Device Plan. While the Device Plan should remain constant, any changes to your Airtime Plan or insurance add-ons can increase your total.

  • Family Plan additions

Review your add-ons in My O2 and remove any you no longer need.

6. Mid-Contract Price Rises

O2, like most networks, includes annual price increase clauses in their contracts. These are typically linked to inflation (RPI or CPI) plus a fixed percentage. The 2024 increase was approximately 8-9% for many customers.

Your Rights:

If the increase exceeds the terms outlined in your original contract, you may have the right to leave without penalty. Check your contract terms carefully.

7. One-Off Charges and Activation Fees

Certain actions trigger one-time charges that can inflate a single month's bill:

  • Early upgrade fees
  • Device delivery charges
  • Connection fees for new lines

  • Paper bill charges (if not opted for online billing)

Using your phone as a mobile hotspot (tethering) consumes data from your allowance. Heavy tethering can quickly exhaust your data, leading to out-of-bundle charges. Some older O2 plans had specific tethering limits—check if yours does.

  • Discounts not applied
  • Charges for services you cancelled

How to Get Your O2 PAC Code in 60 Seconds

Last Updated: January 2025

Switching mobile networks while keeping your cherished phone number shouldn't be complicated. If you're leaving O2 and want to take your number with you, you'll need a PAC (Porting Authorisation Code). The good news? Getting your O2 PAC code is now incredibly simple and can be done in under a minute.

In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through every method to obtain your PAC code, explain how to use it, and answer all your questions about keeping your number when switching from O2.

What Is a PAC Code?

A PAC (Porting Authorisation Code) is a unique nine-character code that authorises your new network to transfer your existing mobile number from O2. Without it, you'll be assigned a new number when you switch—which means updating everyone in your contacts and potentially missing important calls.

Key Facts About PAC Codes:
  • PAC codes are valid for 30 days from issue
  • They're completely free to request
  • You can get one even if you're still in contract (though exit fees may apply)
  • Your O2 service continues until the port is complete

Method 1: Text to Switch (Fastest - 60 Seconds)

The quickest way to get your O2 PAC code is using the text-to-switch service introduced by Ofcom:

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Open your messaging app on the O2 phone number you want to keep
  2. Create a new text message
  3. Type: PAC
  4. Send to: 65075
  5. Wait for the reply (usually arrives within 60 seconds)

The reply text will contain your nine-character PAC code, along with important information about any early termination fees or outstanding charges on your account.

What the Text Reply Includes:

  • Your unique PAC code (e.g., ABC123456)
  • Expiry date (30 days from issue)
  • Any early termination charges
  • Outstanding device plan balance (if applicable)
  • Instructions for your new provider

Method 2: My O2 Online Account

If you prefer managing things online, you can request your PAC through the My O2 portal:

  1. Visit o2.co.uk and sign in to My O2
  2. Navigate to "My details" or "Account settings"
  3. Select "Request your PAC" or "Leaving O2"
  4. Follow the on-screen instructions
  5. Your PAC will be displayed on screen and sent via text

Method 3: Call O2 Directly

For those who prefer speaking to someone:

  • Call 202 from your O2 mobile
  • Or call 0344 809 0202 from any other phone
  • Follow the automated options or ask for "PAC code"
  • Verify your identity with security questions
  • Request your PAC code

Note: This method takes longer due to potential wait times, but it's useful if you have questions about your account.

How to Use Your O2 PAC Code

Once you have your PAC code, here's how to complete the switch:

Step Action Timeline
1 Order your new SIM or phone from your chosen network Before giving PAC
2 Activate your new SIM when it arrives Upon delivery
3 Provide your PAC code to your new network Within 30 days
4 Choose your switch date (usually next working day) When providing PAC
5 Number transfers (you may lose service briefly) On switch day
6 Your old O2 service automatically cancels After successful port

Important Things to Remember

Don't Cancel Your O2 Contract Early

Your O2 contract will be cancelled automatically when your number transfers. If you cancel manually before the port completes, you risk losing your number permanently.

Keep Your O2 SIM Active

Until the transfer is complete, keep your O2 SIM in your phone. You'll receive confirmation texts about the switch, and you need to be reachable in case of any issues.

Backup Your Contacts and Data

While your number transfers, your contacts, photos, and other data don't. Ensure everything is backed up to the cloud or your computer before switching.

Troubleshooting Common PAC Code Issues

"My PAC code has expired"

PAC codes expire after 30 days. Simply request a new one using any of the methods above.

"My new network says the PAC is invalid"

Double-check you've entered all nine characters correctly. PAC codes contain both letters and numbers. If problems persist, contact O2 to verify the code.

"I have multiple numbers on my O2 account"

The text-to-switch service only works for the number you're texting from. For additional numbers, use My O2 or call customer service.

"I'm still in contract—can I get a PAC?"

Yes, you can request a PAC at any time. However, O2 will inform you of any early termination fees you'll need to pay.

What If You Don't Want to Keep Your Number?

If you're happy to get a new number when you switch, request a STAC (Service Termination Authorisation Code) instead:

  • Text STAC to 75075
  • This terminates your O2 service without keeping your number
  • Useful if you're getting unwanted calls or want a fresh start

O2 Signal Not Working? Complete Troubleshooting Guide

Last Updated: January 2025

There's nothing more frustrating than reaching for your phone only to discover you have no signal. Whether it's a complete signal blackout, intermittent drops, or poor call quality, O2 signal problems can disrupt your daily life and leave you feeling disconnected.

This comprehensive troubleshooting guide will walk you through every possible solution—from quick fixes you can try right now to knowing when it's time to contact O2 for professional support.

Quick Diagnostic: What Type of Signal Problem Do You Have?

Before diving into solutions, identify your specific issue:

Problem Type Symptoms Likely Cause
No Signal No bars, "No Service" message Network outage, SIM issue, phone problem
Weak Signal 1-2 bars, dropped calls Location, building interference, antenna issue
Data Only Issues Calls work, internet doesn't APN settings, data limit, 4G/5G settings
Intermittent Drops Signal comes and goes Network congestion, phone software, SIM fault

Immediate Fixes to Try First

1. Toggle Airplane Mode

The oldest trick in the book—and it works surprisingly often:

  1. Swipe down from the top of your screen (Android) or swipe up from bottom (iPhone)
  2. Tap the airplane icon to enable Airplane Mode
  3. Wait 10 seconds
  4. Tap the airplane icon again to disable
  5. Wait for your phone to reconnect to the network

This forces your phone to re-establish its connection to O2's network, often resolving temporary glitches.

2. Restart Your Phone

A simple restart clears temporary software issues:

  • Hold down the power button
  • Select "Restart" or "Reboot" (not just power off)
  • Wait for full restart and check signal

3. Check for Network Outages

Before spending time troubleshooting, verify if O2 is experiencing a known outage:

  • Visit O2's service status page
  • Check DownDetector for O2 reports
  • Ask friends or family on O2 if they're experiencing issues
  • Check O2's Twitter account for outage announcements

4. Remove and Reinsert Your SIM Card

A loose or dirty SIM can cause connection problems:

  1. Power off your phone completely
  2. Locate your SIM tray (usually on the side of the phone)
  3. Use the SIM ejector tool to remove the tray
  4. Check the SIM for visible damage or dirt
  5. Gently clean with a soft, dry cloth if needed
  6. Reinsert the SIM firmly and power on

Advanced Troubleshooting Steps

5. Reset Network Settings

If basic fixes don't work, resetting network settings often resolves deeper issues:

iPhone:

  1. Go to Settings > General > Transfer or Reset iPhone
  2. Tap "Reset" then "Reset Network Settings"
  3. Enter your passcode if prompted
  4. Confirm the reset

Android:

  1. Go to Settings > General Management > Reset
  2. Tap "Reset Network Settings"
  3. Confirm the reset
Warning: This will remove all saved Wi-Fi passwords and Bluetooth pairings. Have your Wi-Fi password handy to reconnect.

6. Check and Update APN Settings

Incorrect APN (Access Point Name) settings can prevent data connectivity:

Correct O2 APN Settings:

  • Name: O2 Internet
  • APN: mobile.o2.co.uk
  • Username: o2web
  • Password: password
  • MMSC: http://mmsc.mms.o2.co.uk:8002
  • MCC: 234
  • MNC: 10

7. Check for Software Updates

Outdated software can cause network compatibility issues:

  • iPhone: Settings > General > Software Update
  • Android: Settings > Software Update (varies by manufacturer)

8. Try a Different Location

Building materials can significantly affect signal strength:

  • Move near a window
  • Go outside
  • Try a different floor of your building
  • Test in an open area away from tall buildings

Location-Specific Signal Issues

At Home

If you consistently have poor signal at home:

  • O2 TU Go: Use Wi-Fi calling through the TU Go app
  • 4G Calling: Enable VoLTE in your phone settings
  • Signal Booster: Contact O2 about a free signal booster

At Work

Office buildings often have signal-blocking materials:

  • Ask your employer about a mobile signal repeater
  • Use Wi-Fi calling if available
  • Identify signal "sweet spots" in the building

While Travelling

Signal varies significantly across the UK:

  • Check O2's coverage map before travelling
  • Download offline maps for navigation
  • Enable data roaming if appropriate

When to Contact O2

If you've tried all the above steps and still experience O2 signal problems, it's time to contact O2 directly:

  • Persistent no-signal issues in multiple locations
  • Signal problems following a SIM swap or phone change
  • Sudden signal degradation in an area that previously worked
  • Suspected network fault or mast issue

Compensation for Signal Issues

If you've experienced significant service disruption, you may be entitled to compensation:

  • Document the dates and times of outages
  • Keep records of any costs incurred (e.g., purchasing alternative services)
  • Contact O2 to request a service credit
  • Escalate to CISAS if O2 refuses reasonable compensation

O2 signal problems can usually be resolved with the troubleshooting steps outlined in this guide. Start with the quick fixes—airplane mode toggle, restart, and SIM reinsertion—before moving on to more advanced solutions. Remember that network coverage varies by location, and some areas may have inherent limitations.

If problems persist across multiple locations or started suddenly, don't hesitate to contact O2. As a paying customer, you deserve reliable service, and O2 has obligations to maintain network quality and address reported issues.

If you're considering a new mobile contract with O2 or are already a customer trying to understand your bill, you've likely encountered O2 Refresh. This unique tariff structure sets O2 apart from competitors, but it can also be confusing for customers used to traditional mobile contracts.

In this comprehensive guide, we'll demystify O2 Refresh, explain exactly how it works, compare it to standard contracts, and help you decide whether it's the right choice for your mobile needs.

What Is O2 Refresh?

O2 Refresh is O2's signature tariff that splits your monthly mobile bill into two distinct parts:

  1. Device Plan: The cost of your physical phone or tablet
  2. Airtime Plan: The cost of your calls, texts, and data allowance

This separation offers flexibility that traditional combined contracts simply can't match.

How O2 Refresh Works: A Detailed Breakdown

The Device Plan

Your Device Plan covers the actual cost of your handset. Here's what you need to know:

  • Typically runs for 24, 36, or 48 months
  • Monthly cost depends on the phone model and upfront payment
  • Interest-free credit agreement
  • Once paid off, you own the phone outright

The Airtime Plan

Your Airtime Plan is essentially a SIM-only contract:

  • Includes your monthly data, minutes, and texts allowance
  • Can be changed once per month (upgrade or downgrade)
  • Continues even after your Device Plan ends
  • Available in various tiers to suit different usage patterns

O2 Refresh vs Traditional Contracts: The Key Differences

Feature O2 Refresh Traditional Contract
Bill Structure Separate Device + Airtime Single combined payment
Early Upgrade Cost Pay off Device Plan only Pay off entire remaining contract
Post-Contract Bill Automatically reduces (Auto-Save) Stays the same unless you act
Tariff Flexibility Change monthly Usually fixed for contract term
Phone Ownership Yours after Device Plan ends Varies by contract type

The Auto-Save Advantage

One of O2 Refresh's standout features is Auto-Save:

What Is Auto-Save?
Once you've paid off your Device Plan (typically after 24-48 months), O2 automatically reduces your monthly bill to just the Airtime Plan cost. You'll never continue paying for a phone you already own.

Example:

  • Monthly bill: £45 (£25 Device + £20 Airtime)
  • After 24 months: Bill drops to £20 (Airtime only)
  • Annual saving: £300

This contrasts sharply with traditional contracts where many customers continue paying the full amount indefinitely unless they actively contact their provider.

Early Upgrades with O2 Refresh

Want the latest phone before your contract ends? O2 Refresh makes this more affordable:

Standard Early Upgrade:

  1. Pay off your remaining Device Plan balance
  2. Your Airtime Plan continues or can be changed
  3. Start a new Device Plan with your chosen phone

O2 Switch Up:

For an additional monthly fee, O2 Switch Up allows you to swap your phone every 90 days without paying off your current Device Plan. O2 settles the remaining balance for you.

Is O2 Refresh Worth It? Pros and Cons

Advantages of O2 Refresh

  • Cost savings after contract ends – Auto-Save reduces your bill automatically
  • Cheaper early upgrades – Pay off device only, not full contract
  • Flexible tariffs – Change your Airtime Plan monthly
  • Transparency – Clear separation of phone and service costs
  • Ownership – Phone is yours once Device Plan ends

Disadvantages of O2 Refresh

  • Higher monthly costs initially – Can appear more expensive than competitors
  • Complexity – Two-part bills can be confusing

How to Get Compensation from O2 for Network Issues

When your mobile service fails—whether through network outages, billing errors, or unfulfilled promises—it doesn't just cause inconvenience; it can cost you money, missed opportunities, and significant frustration. 1. Network Outages and Service Disruption

If O2's network experiences significant downtime that affects your ability to use your phone:

  • Complete loss of service for extended periods
  • Widespread outages affecting multiple customers
  • Repeated intermittent service issues
  • Data connectivity failures preventing essential use
Your Rights: Ofcom guidance states that providers should offer compensation for service failures that are their fault and cause significant inconvenience. While there's no automatic entitlement, you have a strong case for reasonable compensation.

2. Billing Errors and Overcharging

When O2 charges you incorrectly:

  • Charges for services you didn't use or authorise
  • Incorrect tariff applied to your account
  • Promised discounts not applied
  • Double-charging for the same service
  • Charges after contract cancellation

3. Mis-Selling and Broken Promises

If O2 staff made commitments that weren't honoured:

  • Promised coverage that doesn't exist in your area
  • Guaranteed speeds that aren't delivered
  • Promised discounts or credits not applied
  • Misleading information about contract terms

4. Delayed or Failed Deliveries

When your new phone or SIM doesn't arrive as promised:

  • Significant delays beyond promised delivery dates
  • Failed deliveries due to O2's error
  • Incorrect items sent

5. Poor Repair Service

If your phone repair wasn't handled properly:

  • Excessive repair times
  • Device returned unfixed or with new issues
  • Damage caused during repair
  • Loss of data due to O2's handling
